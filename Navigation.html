Making Buttons

Making buttons with Mr. Chiu. Using fireworks we were able to make buttons and these buttons include over and down gradients. When not clicked it can be a bit lighter and when clicked it will be darker. This allows us to know which page we are in and which button we have just recently clicked.

Using fireworks its a very five minute simple step. Most of it is just part of a design, however one major step that allows the custom handmade box into a button is really what just does its magic.

Using options such as over and down allow you to add things like hover view and you can see different colours when hovered over. Clicking it will also allow you to view different things. Depends on your preferences and settings.

Planning the Buttons and Navigation Elements.

There are 5 links and 5 titles that need to be created

The look and feel has to be consistent with the look and feel of the webpage. The buttons must seem to belong with the rest of the webpage. If they seem out of contrast or size then consistency will not be maintained. Users might get distracted for their purpose and lose interest.

After reading the article on important webpage principles from pickthebrain, I have found that it is important to take every step think like a user and then design because it will make the webpage grow more into what a future expected uesr would want it to be like. Moreover, I learnt that keeping it simple with simplistic elements such as photos would be better than high graphic photos that take time to load. Do not test the patience of the user because he won't feel its professional or matching the standard he expected. Always try to meet the users expectations as much as possible.

Navigation

The navigation bar will be just below the logo. This will allow users to straight away get into their purpose for what they visited their site. So it will be relatively around top middle of the webpage.

On the navigation bar the contexts will be buttons that help users navigate through vague purposes they could possible interested. Dont worry, I know vague is not good, however this will allow users to enter where they are interested and view their choice in much more detail. There will be four buttons that resemble four choices. The context are, services, gallery, heritage, and contact us.

The navigation bar will be on every page because this will allow users to quickly run through pages without going back to home. Moreover, they can go to any other page and go to home anytime. I believe this will allow users to save time and find their purpose for visiting their webiste much quicker. This also allows to maintain consistency and keep them feeling belonged without losing interest.

The navigation bar will allow users to know which page they are on. This is because the button they click on through the navigation bar will allow the user to visit their subject on interest will be slighly highlighted or shaded. If not highligted or shaded it will be illusioned to look like it has been pressed. This is a really good illusion because it shows that the user has literally pressed that button and they are currently on that site.

Graphical Navigation

Buttons were made in Fireworks. The buttons were made by simple box shapes that were retouched into retangles. Then simply text were added into the shapes. Once a template is created for one button, all the other ones can be created with ease following the same format.

To distinguish between which buttons the user is trying to access, not active, or in the process of accesing there are different states that can be set.

Different styles can be added to different states. For example when it will be up and down or possibly raised.

Similarily you can also disinguish a hover, the button will shift to the right and the colour will be highlighted (decrease in opacity).

Textual Navigation

After linking the targeted site to the text, the navigation button is in place already. But rather than changing the appearance, we change the color of the text to distinguish the different states.

I have created a navigation button for "Home" at the top of this site. When unvisited and clicked on it appears gray. When visited, it turns lighter.

Compare and Contrast

I feel that my graphical navigation fits much more flawlessly, compared to my textual navigation.

Better to use Graphical navigation because...

More visual appeal to the users due to the changes in "texture" of the buttons. By texture I mean how it can sink in, highlight/change in colour, and the different states such as raise.

Grabs more attention that its there and it exits due to the more space being occupied by the button itself. It will also most probably be present in most pages, making it a part of the websitse permanent look and feel.

Neccessary content that users would need or want to access that naviation button for more than once to access the information to again to perhaps reread the information.

Textual Navigation would be better when...

It is a supplementary link that users might have to access for further infomration- maybe for an example or evidence of the content on the site.

A button would not be neccessary because users probably would only need to visit it once.

Do not want to distract users from the content
